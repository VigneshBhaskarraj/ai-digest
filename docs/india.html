VC Trends 6 / 10
Global AI funds supersizing — and India is in the crosshairs

Menlo Ventures raising $3B (its largest ever) specifically for AI startups follows a broader pattern of established US VCs building war chests for the AI cycle. For Indian founders, this means more dollars chasing fewer quality AI deals — seed and Series A valuations in AI will keep inflating. Founders with enterprise AI traction should move fast on term sheets.

SEBI's GARUDA framework unlocks AIF flexibility for startup investors

SEBI's new GARUDA framework eases structural bottlenecks for Alternative Investment Funds, which are a primary vehicle for domestic VC investment in Indian startups. This is a meaningful regulatory tailwind — expect more domestic capital deployment into early-stage AI and deeptech as fund managers face fewer compliance frictions.

VC definition of success has shifted from growth-at-all-costs to capital efficiency

The era of 100+ unicorns on blitzscaling logic (2015–2021) is definitively over. VCs are now rewarding founders who show margin discipline and path-to-profitability alongside AI-driven product differentiation. For AI startups specifically, this means revenue quality and gross margin — not just ARR growth — are now the metrics that unlock the next round.

Policy Watch 7 / 10
Reliance Jio's 'Hey Jio' Network-Level AI Agent Raises Privacy Red Flags

Jio's AI agent, activated via 'Hey Jio' during calls, processes user voice data at the network level — raising unresolved questions about data storage, consent, and privacy compliance under India's DPDP Act. This is a critical regulatory grey zone: if TRAI or MeitY moves to set guardrails on telecom-layer AI agents, it will set precedent for how all on-device and network AI is governed in India.
