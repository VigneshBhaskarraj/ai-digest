VC Trends 6 / 9
H1 2026 Funding Contraction: Quality Over Quantity

Indian startup funding fell 9% to $5.2Bn in H1 2026 despite investor interest remaining broadly intact — signaling that VCs are concentrating bets on fewer, higher-conviction deals rather than spraying capital. For AI founders, this means differentiated tech and clear unit economics are non-negotiable for raising in H2 2026.

AI Infrastructure Demand Fueling Hardware & Semiconductor Bets

Dell now manufactures the majority of its servers in India to meet surging local AI demand, while the Finance Ministry has cleared a ₹1.25 lakh crore outlay for India Semiconductor Mission 2.0. VCs and the government are converging on the view that AI compute infrastructure — chips, servers, data centers — is the next critical investable layer in India's tech stack.

Fintech Follow-Ons Signal Resilience in B2B Infrastructure

SMBC's follow-on across three fintech verticals (housing finance, supply chain, collections) shows that B2B fintech infrastructure continues to attract institutional capital even in a softer macro environment. AI-enabled underwriting, collections automation, and trade finance remain attractive sub-sectors for founders building in this space.

Policy Watch 7 / 9
India Pivots to Open-Source & Homegrown AI to Counter Frontier Model Access Restrictions

MeitY is actively championing open-source AI models and indigenous development as a strategic hedge against potential export controls or access curbs on frontier models from the US and other geographies. This directly accelerates funding and policy tailwinds for Indian AI labs, open-source model startups, and inference infrastructure players.
