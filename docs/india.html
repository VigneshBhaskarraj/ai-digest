VC Trends 4 / 8
AI Self-Reliance as Investment Thesis

The US directive ordering Anthropic to disable Fable 5 and Mythos AI models has catalyzed a sharp conversation in India Inc about sovereign AI infrastructure. Indian technology leaders are publicly calling for domestic alternatives, which signals an emerging VC thesis around India-built foundational AI models and infrastructure plays. Founders building Indian LLMs, AI inference stacks, or enterprise-grade domestic AI tools are now operating in a significantly more favorable narrative environment.

Steady VC Inflow But Cautious Momentum

The June 6–12 weekly funding roundup shows $243 Mn raised across 25 startups — a healthy number, but analysts caution this is unlikely to build into a strong funding surge given broader macro uncertainty. VCs appear selective, backing deep tech (biotech, AI, renewables) over consumer plays, suggesting founders should expect rigorous due diligence and longer close cycles even as deal volume holds up.

Judgment Over Tool Proficiency in AI Hiring

At DevSparks Bengaluru 2026, leaders from Zoho and Toast emphasized that employers now value judgment, adaptability, and real-world problem-solving over AI tool familiarity. This is a signal for B2B AI startups: buyers are maturing and will pay for outcomes, not demos — and hiring for AI product teams should prioritize domain expertise over prompt engineering credentials.
