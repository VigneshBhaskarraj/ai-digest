VC Trends 10 / 13
Agentic AI is driving M&A, not just fundraising

MoEngage's acquisition of Aampe to scale 'agentic marketing' is a signal that established Indian SaaS players are buying AI capabilities rather than building them. Founders building narrow, high-quality AI agents in martech, fintech, and enterprise workflows are now acquisition targets — not just VC targets. Expect more bolt-on AI acquisitions from India's SaaS mid-caps in H2 2026.

Compute access and AI infrastructure heating up globally — India must move fast

Meta and Microsoft are locking up $850B+ in data center leases, OpenAI is launching custom silicon (Jalapeno with Broadcom), and a16z just backed a compute marketplace at $33M. The global AI infrastructure arms race is accelerating, and India's window to build sovereign compute capacity is narrowing. VCs backing Indian infra plays — from cheaper AI chips to spacetech data links — are positioning early on a theme that will dominate the next 3 years.

India's AI talent crisis is becoming a structural investment risk

Multiple pieces today flag the same issue: India is building AI infrastructure but struggling to retain or repatriate AI talent. For VCs underwriting Indian AI startups, this is no longer a soft concern — it's a diligence question. Startups that build talent moats early (through research labs, ESOP structures, or diaspora hiring) will command a premium; those that don't will hit a ceiling fast.

Policy Watch 11 / 13
CISF Proposes Linking Airport Facial Recognition Cameras to NATGRID

CISF is pushing to connect facial recognition data from 1.5 lakh CCTVs across airports, metros, ports, and government buildings to a proposed data fusion centre in Delhi tied to NATGRID. This is one of the most significant expansions of AI-powered surveillance infrastructure in India's history — and it will create both procurement opportunities and serious civil liberties pushback that AI startups operating in identity and computer vision must monitor closely.
