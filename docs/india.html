VC Trends 6 / 10
Semiconductor infrastructure gets serious VC attention

Calligo's $12-15M raise from US-based BIG Capital marks a shift: VCs are finally betting on India's silicon layer, not just software. RISC-V and energy-efficient AI chips are the new frontier. For founders: hardware is back, but you need global capital and deep technical moats — domestic VCs remain cautious on capex-heavy semiconductor plays.

Late-stage deals dominate, seed-stage founders struggle

April's $660M was driven by late-stage rounds for established names like KreditBee and Palmonas, while early-stage deal count remains thin. VCs are de-risking by backing proven models in fintech and SaaS. Seed founders in AI should expect tougher diligence and longer fundraising cycles — product-market fit is now table stakes, not a milestone.

State governments compete to build deep-tech ecosystems

Karnataka's SpaceTech CoE launch signals a new playbook: state-level infrastructure investing to attract startups and talent. Expect Tamil Nadu, Telangana, and Maharashtra to announce similar sector-specific CoEs in semiconductors, AI, and quantum. Founders should leverage these platforms for subsidized R&D and pilot partnerships — but watch for bureaucratic overhead.
