VC Trends 8 / 12
Heritage & Vernacular AI: Investors Eye India-Specific Foundation Layer

Articul8's $30–50M raise for Sanskrit and traditional knowledge AI is the clearest signal yet that global investors see 'India-native' AI as a distinct category, not just a cost-arbitrage play. This follows a broader pattern of bets on Indic language models and cultural knowledge graphs. Founders building AI on top of India's unique linguistic, legal, or epistemic heritage should note that the funding environment is now receptive.

New Domestic Funds Fill the Growth-Stage Gap

Ashish Dave's Sanskrit Capital (₹700–1,000 Cr) targeting Series A/B is one of several new India-focused funds launching in 2026, as former institutional investors go independent. This is a structural shift — more domestic LP capital is being channelled into growth-stage startups, reducing founder dependence on foreign funds for bridge rounds. AI startups crossing ₹10–20 Cr ARR should actively court this new cohort of funds.

Vertical AI + Niche B2B Continues to Attract Tier-1 Backing

Lightspeed's Series A in Sandstone (legal OS) and Articul8's heritage AI both underscore that top-tier VCs are moving decisively into vertical AI plays — not generalist copilots. The message for founders is clear: deep domain specificity, not breadth, is what's getting term sheets right now in India's AI market.
