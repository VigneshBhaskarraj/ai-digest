VC Trends 6 / 10
VC deal flow halves — macro headwinds, not sector fatigue

Venture and PE investments crashed to $2.7B across just 38 deals in April, roughly half of recent monthly averages, with rupee depreciation cited as a key sentiment dampener. This is a macro signal, not a vote against AI — founders raising now face tougher valuation conversations and longer due diligence cycles. Deals that do close are concentrated in deep-tech and consumer services with clear unit economics.

India's ₹12 trillion data centre build-out is necessary but not sufficient

India is committing massive infrastructure capital to AI data centres, but experts are flagging a critical gap: semiconductor investment has collapsed and the talent pipeline for advanced AI workloads remains thin. VCs watching this space should note that the real opportunity — and moat — will belong to startups that can bridge the infrastructure-to-application gap, not just provision compute.

GCCs reshaping India's AI talent market — creating both risk and opportunity

Global Capability Centres are rethinking hiring in India as AI shifts skill demand toward advanced ML, agentic AI, and systems thinking, away from traditional IT services roles. This is creating a talent surplus in legacy skills and a fierce war for AI-native engineers. For Indian AI startups, this is a double-edged sword: talent availability may improve for well-funded teams, but competition from GCC compensation packages remains brutal.

Policy Watch 7 / 10
MeitY IT Rules amendments expected by end of May

India's Ministry of Electronics and IT is expected to notify final amendments to the IT Rules by month-end, with ongoing concerns about powers granted to an Internet Disputes Committee (IDC) and the regulation of user-generated news content. For AI startups operating content platforms or building generative AI products in India, these rules could directly affect content moderation obligations and liability frameworks.
