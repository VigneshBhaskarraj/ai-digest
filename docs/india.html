VC Trends 4 / 7
AI Startups Re-Rating Fast — Rocket's 3x Valuation Jump in Under a Year

Rocket AI going from a $15M Salesforce-backed round to a $40–50M raise in less than 12 months is a concrete data point showing that VCs are aggressively re-pricing AI software companies upward. For founders, the window to raise on strong terms is open — but execution proof matters more than ever. Investors are betting on AI companies that can demonstrate real enterprise traction, not just demos.

AI Rewiring India's B2C Growth Stack — But Enterprise Agent Readiness Lags

Two signals are colliding: AI is actively reshaping how India's consumer-facing companies (Zomato, Meesho, etc.) target and acquire customers, yet enterprises are struggling to deploy AI agents at scale due to data readiness and integration gaps. This creates a dual opportunity — AI tooling for B2C growth (marketing, personalization) is monetizing faster than B2B agentic workflows. Founders building for India's consumer internet layer have a clearer near-term path to revenue.

Macquarie's 'Rolling Bubbles' Warning — A Calibration Signal for Indian AI Investors

Macquarie's prediction that the global AI boom will unwind through sequential sector-by-sector corrections — not a single crash — is a useful framework for India's VC market. It implies that some AI verticals (e.g., coding assistants, generic LLM wrappers) may deflate before others (e.g., AI in fintech, healthcare, logistics). Indian VCs should pressure-test whether their portfolio companies have defensible moats or are riding a wave about to roll over.
