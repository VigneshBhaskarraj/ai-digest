VC Trends 5 / 8
Micro VCs stepping up as critical early-stage backers for deep tech

A YourStory analysis highlights that Micro VCs are becoming the essential first-lap runners for capital-intensive sectors — deep tech, hardware, biotech — where long R&D cycles before market signal scare off larger funds. For AI founders building infrastructure or applied AI with long development horizons, micro VCs are now the logical first call. This structural role is hardening as India's venture relay matures.

May 2026 funding momentum signals a recovering deal market

India's $1.11 billion raised in May — up 37% YoY — is a strong signal that institutional appetite is back after a cautious 2025. While Rapido's $240M skews the headline number, the 14-deal week ending May 25 shows breadth, not just size. Early-stage founders should read this as a window of VC receptivity opening up.

AI-driven workforce disruption forcing enterprise restructuring bets

Both EY and ACCA released reports flagging AI's imminent reshaping of India's IT services job market — the most lucrative white-collar pipeline in the country. VCs are likely to accelerate bets on AI upskilling, workforce intelligence, and automation tooling as enterprises scramble to redesign roles. India's higher AI confidence vs global peers (per ACCA) also signals a domestic market primed for AI-native HR and productivity tools.

Policy Watch 6 / 8
Delhi HC rules Google liable for trademark infringement in keyword advertising

The Delhi High Court fined Google ₹30 lakh for allowing competitors to bid on Hindware's trademarked keywords, setting a precedent that could constrain Big Tech's ad-monetization practices in India. For Indian AI and SaaS startups that have long complained about predatory keyword squatting by larger rivals, this ruling opens a legal avenue to protect brand equity in performance marketing.
