VC Trends 7 / 11
AI Infrastructure: Edge-to-Cloud Compute Gets Funded

Pavestone's $5M bet on Tsavorite signals VCs are backing full-stack AI compute infrastructure that solves the power-efficiency problem. With chipmakers like Cerebras targeting $26.6B valuations globally, Indian AI hardware startups building custom silicon (OPUs integrating CPU/GPU/memory) are positioned to capture enterprise demand for scalable, cost-effective AI workloads. Founders building chips or compute orchestration layers should emphasize power efficiency and composability — these are the new moats.

Space-Tech Meets AI: Sovereign AI Goes Orbital

Pixxel and Sarvam's orbital data centre partnership is the first signal that India's AI stack is expanding vertically into space. Processing hyperspectral imaging data in orbit using large language models eliminates latency and ground dependency — a critical advantage for defense, agriculture, and climate monitoring. This follows the Agnikul Cosmos-Neev Cloud playbook. VCs should watch for more spacetech-AI convergence plays: data sovereignty, real-time inference, and edge compute are no longer Earth-bound concepts.

Sovereign AI Hardware Push: NVIDIA DGX Spark and Local LLMs

NVIDIA's Megh Makwana demonstrated running LLMs on portable DGX Spark devices at an India event, emphasizing quality data over larger models. This aligns with India's sovereign AI narrative: localized compute, India-specific datasets, and lower dependency on cloud hyperscalers. For founders, the message is clear — invest in proprietary datasets and efficient models before scaling to frontier LLMs. VCs are watching for startups building India-first AI tooling and data infrastructure.
