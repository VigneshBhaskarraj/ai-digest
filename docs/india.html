📈 Where VCs Are Focusing

📈 Deep tech and semiconductor bets are accelerating

Calligo's $12-15M raise for RISC-V chips and growing interest in indigenous semiconductor plays signal VCs are betting on hardware-software convergence for AI workloads. This aligns with India's semiconductor mission and represents a shift from pure software plays — expect more capital flowing into chip design, edge AI, and infrastructure startups that can capture value in the AI stack below the model layer.

📈 Enterprise AI infrastructure is commanding mega-rounds

Netomi's $110M Series C for AI customer service and Kimbal's $22M for smart grid tech show VCs are writing large checks for B2B AI infrastructure with clear ROI paths. The through-line: startups automating high-cost enterprise functions (customer support, energy management) are raising at scale, while consumer AI remains capital-constrained. Indian founders should focus on enterprise pain points with measurable cost savings.
