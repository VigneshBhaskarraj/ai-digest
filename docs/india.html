VC Trends 5 / 8
Indigenous semiconductor and AI infrastructure plays attracting serious capital

Calligo's $12-15M raise and the Portkey acquisition signal VCs are finally backing India's chip and AI infra layer — not just SaaS wrappers. With government semiconductor incentives and enterprise AI demand converging, investors see a rare window to build defensible, capital-intensive businesses in India. Founders building at the infrastructure level (chips, AI agents, dev tooling) should expect faster conversations with growth-stage funds.

Enterprise AI automation crossing $100M+ funding milestones

Netomi's $110M Series C and Portkey's acquisition by Palo Alto Networks show that AI-native enterprise solutions — especially in customer service, security, and workflow automation — are reaching late-stage validation. VCs are betting big on companies that can plug AI directly into Fortune 500 ops, not just demo GPT wrappers. If you're building for the enterprise, your go-to-market and integration story matters more than your model choice.
