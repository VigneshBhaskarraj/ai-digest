VC Trends 5 / 9
Seed cheque sizes are doubling — and VCs are getting pickier

Indian VCs are writing significantly larger seed and early-stage cheques, with average round sizes nearly doubling. The catch: selectivity is rising in parallel, meaning fewer deals but more capital per winner. For founders, this is a barbell market — top-decile teams raise more easily than ever, while median-quality decks face harder closes.

Late-stage confidence is back — $900M CRED round resets the benchmark

CRED's $900M raise — single-handedly lifting India's weekly startup funding to $1.09Bn (up 290% year-on-year) — signals that global LPs are re-engaging with Indian late-stage bets after two years of caution. If this momentum holds, expect more growth-stage rounds in fintech, AI infra, and consumer platforms in H2 2026.

Global AI platforms doubling down on India as a strategic market

OpenAI's appointment of a dedicated India MD (Prabhjeet Singh, former Uber India head) signals that global AI labs now treat India as a Tier-1 commercial priority, not just an engineering hub. This will intensify competition for Indian AI startups in enterprise and developer tooling segments where OpenAI will now actively push.

Policy Watch 6 / 9
India scraps 20% local sourcing rule for Satcom, retains security guardrails

India has dropped the 20% local sourcing requirement for satellite broadband operators like Starlink and Eutelsat OneWeb, clearing a major commercial barrier for global satcom players. For AI startups, wider satellite broadband penetration accelerates rural connectivity — expanding the addressable market for AI-powered services in Tier 3+ markets.
