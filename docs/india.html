Hardware AI bets heating up: Semiconductor plays attracting growth capital

Calligo's $12-15M RISC-V chip raise marks a clear signal VCs are backing indigenous AI hardware, not just software layers. BIG Capital leading from the US suggests global funds see India as a credible semiconductor design hub for energy-efficient AI compute. Founders building at the silicon level should expect more inbound interest if they have defensible IP and clear path to commercial deployment — the semiconductor funding desert is ending.

Late-stage deals still driving volumes; seed remains cautious

April's $660M was dominated by late-stage rounds (KreditBee, Snabbit), while the last week's $219M bounce came from mid-sized deals. This signals VCs are still preferring proven business models with traction over early bets. Seed founders should focus on revenue signals and product-market fit fast — the easy money for idea-stage AI companies isn't back yet, but growth-stage capital is available for those who've crossed ₹5-10 Cr ARR.
