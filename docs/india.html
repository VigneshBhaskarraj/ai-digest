VC Trends 7 / 11
Sovereign AI infrastructure is the new battleground — and capital is flooding in

Sarvam AI's outsized raise and Reliance's AGM commitment to sovereign AI infrastructure signal that India-first foundation model and compute bets are now commanding serious institutional capital. VCs and corporates alike are treating sovereign AI not as a nice-to-have but as a strategic necessity. Founders building Indian-language models, domestic compute stacks, or AI tooling for Bharat-scale use cases should expect premium valuations in 2026.

Global Big Tech eyes India's consumer-tech unicorns as AI distribution plays

Meta's reported interest in CRED at a $4Bn valuation isn't just an M&A story — it's a signal that global AI giants see India's high-intent consumer fintech platforms as distribution moats for AI-native financial services. Founders and VCs building at the consumer-AI intersection should expect more strategic interest from US and global tech players looking for India market entry via acquisition.

Deep-tech India-France corridor opens up as a serious alternative funding pipeline

Bharat Innovates 2026 closing with $254.5M in commitments and 50+ agreements from Nice is not a one-off — it reflects a maturing India-Europe deep-tech funding corridor. For founders in AI, space, and advanced manufacturing who have hit a ceiling on domestic VC, European strategic capital is now a credible and increasingly accessible option.

The Delhi High Court upheld the government's temporary Telegram blocking order under Section 69A of the IT Act, citing bots and mirror channels enabling illegal content — a precedent that gives the Centre broad authority to block platforms at will.
