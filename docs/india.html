VC Trends 4 / 8
Semiconductors and deep-tech hardware attracting serious capital

Calligo's $12-15M raise from a US fund marks a turning point: VCs are now backing India's indigenous chip plays, not just SaaS or consumer tech. With global supply chains rewiring and government PLI incentives kicking in, semiconductor startups building AI-optimized hardware are becoming viable bets. Founders in deep-tech should pitch the geopolitical tailwind — this is no longer just a science project.

Agentic AI traffic forcing platform economics rethink

IndiaMART's decision to stop reporting traffic metrics after bot and agentic AI traffic surged is a canary in the coal mine. AI agents are now browsing, inquiring, and distorting conversion funnels across B2B platforms. VCs evaluating marketplaces and SaaS companies must scrutinize whether growth metrics are human or synthetic — and founders need new KPIs that separate signal from AI noise.

LLM-native business models scaling globally, but India playing catch-up

Meta's Q1 earnings reveal LLM-driven recommendations, ads, and AI agents are now core revenue engines, not experiments. Indian AI startups building recommendation systems, content discovery, or ad-tech should note: the window to compete is narrowing fast. VCs are watching for India-specific LLM applications that can't be trivially cloned by Meta or Google — defensibility matters more than ever.

Policy Watch 5 / 8
Delhi government launching AI partnerships with tech companies and startups

Delhi government plans to partner with tech firms and startups to deploy AI tools across public services — a signal that state governments are moving beyond policy talk to actual procurement and co-development.
