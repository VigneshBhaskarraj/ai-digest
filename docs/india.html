VC Trends 6 / 9
Semiconductor infrastructure bet intensifies

VCs are finally backing India's hardware layer with Calligo's $12-15M raise for indigenous RISC-V chips. This signals a strategic shift from software-only AI plays to full-stack bets on energy-efficient computing infrastructure. For founders: if you're building at the silicon or hardware acceleration layer for AI workloads, this is your moment — investors are hunting for defensible IP below the model layer.

Enterprise AI tooling still commanding mega-rounds

Netomi's $110M Series C shows that despite market uncertainty, enterprise-focused AI infrastructure — especially customer service automation powered by LLMs — continues to attract massive capital from strategic investors like Accenture Ventures. The message for founders: horizontal AI platforms serving multiple verticals with clear ROI metrics remain fundable at scale, but you need enterprise traction and proven unit economics.

Energy and compute efficiency becoming core investment thesis

Both Calligo (energy-efficient chips) and Kimbal (battery storage) raises point to a emerging VC focus: AI's power consumption problem. As model training and inference costs explode, investors are backing solutions that optimize energy usage across the stack. Founders building power-aware AI systems, alternative compute architectures, or energy management for data centers have clear product-market fit ahead.
