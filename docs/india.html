VC Trends 6 / 9
AI infrastructure exits validating early bets

Palo Alto's acquisition of Portkey signals that India's AI tooling layer is mature enough for strategic M&A, not just growth equity. VCs who backed infra early (like Elevation) are seeing liquidity windows open faster than consumer AI plays. For founders: building picks-and-shovels for AI agents is a credible path to exits, especially with security/governance hooks.

Semiconductor sovereignty attracting serious capital

Calligo's $12-15M round from US investors shows that India's chip design ambitions — especially energy-efficient RISC-V for AI workloads — are no longer just policy theater. Foreign VCs are willing to fund hardware, a historically tough category, if it's tied to AI compute bottlenecks. This opens a narrow but real window for deep-tech hardware startups beyond software-as-a-service.

Enterprise AI adoption driving customer service automation bets

Netomi's $110M Series C led by Accenture Ventures highlights where corporate dollars are flowing: agentic AI replacing tier-1 support workflows. Indian startups building vertical AI agents (healthcare, fintech, logistics) should note that enterprise buyers now have budget allocated — the shift from pilot to production is accelerating in 2026.

Policy Watch 7 / 9
India negotiating access to Anthropic's Mythos AI cybersecurity model

India is in talks with the US and Anthropic to access Claude's Mythos model for cyber defense, but no Indian firms are yet part of Project Glasswing — the US-led initiative to secure AI systems against advanced threats. This creates a compliance and strategic access gap for Indian AI startups needing cutting-edge security tooling.
