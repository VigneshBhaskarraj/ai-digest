VC Trends 4 / 8
Agentic AI is India's breakout category — not just hype

OpenAI's Codex adoption in India surging 27x since January 2026 — placing India in the global top 5 — is a hard signal that Indian developers and enterprises are rapidly shifting to agentic workflows. VCs should expect a wave of vertical-specific agentic startups building on top of these platforms. Founders who can own a specific workflow (legal, finance, logistics) with agentic AI have a credible wedge right now.

AI is becoming table stakes for India's consumer internet layer

Meesho's deployment of voice AI 'Vaani' for rural commerce and Google Cloud India bundling Gemini into enterprise deals signals that AI is no longer a standalone bet — it's infrastructure embedded into every consumer and enterprise product. Startups pitching 'AI-powered X' need a sharper moat; VCs are increasingly looking for proprietary data or distribution, not just model wrappers.

Semiconductor ambition is real — India is moving from software to stack

NITI Aayog's $120-150B semiconductor value chain target by 2035 is the clearest government signal that India intends to climb the hardware stack. This creates a long-horizon investment thesis for deeptech VCs and opens the door for fabless chip design startups, EDA tools, and semiconductor IP plays — areas historically underfunded in India.

Policy Watch 5 / 8
NITI Aayog: India must build $120-150B semiconductor value chain by 2035

NITI Aayog has set an ambitious target for India to develop a $120-150 billion domestic semiconductor value chain within the next decade, requiring significant investment in manufacturing, design, and ecosystem capabilities. For AI startups, this is a critical upstream signal — domestic chip supply chains will reduce India's AI infrastructure dependence on imports and could unlock government-backed capital for hardware-adjacent AI ventures.
