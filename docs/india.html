VC Trends 6 / 9
Semiconductor infrastructure bets heating up in India

Calligo's $12-15M raise for RISC-V chips signals VCs are moving beyond chip design services into actual silicon manufacturing and energy-efficient computing for AI workloads. This is a capital-intensive, long-cycle bet — expect more infrastructure plays as India pushes semiconductor self-reliance and AI compute becomes a national priority. Founders building at the silicon or compute layer should expect heightened VC interest but stricter due diligence on technical moats and go-to-market timelines.

Enterprise AI services commanding massive Series C rounds

Netomi's $110M Series C from Accenture Ventures underscores that enterprise AI — especially LLM-powered customer service and automation — is no longer experimental. VCs are writing large checks for companies with proven enterprise traction and AI-native products that drive measurable ROI. For Indian founders, this validates building verticalized AI agents and tools for global enterprise customers, not just consumer chatbots.

Seed funding still flowing to infrastructure and immersive tech

Ctruh's $2.5M seed for XR tools and Kimbal's $22M Series B for smart grids show investors backing infrastructure enablers — both physical (energy grids) and digital (immersive enterprise tools). India's startup funding isn't just concentrated in generative AI; there's appetite for picks-and-shovels plays that support AI deployment, renewable energy, and next-gen interfaces. Early-stage founders should position infrastructure plays as AI-adjacent or AI-enabling to attract capital.
