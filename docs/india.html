VC Trends 4 / 6
VC deal volume softens in early May — quality over quantity phase

The week of May 2-8 saw a decline in both deal count and total VC inflow into Indian startups, with no blockbuster AI-specific round to anchor sentiment. This follows a broader pattern of investors becoming more selective post-2025 correction. Founders should expect longer due diligence cycles and stronger pressure to show revenue traction before closing rounds.

AI-generated code becoming table stakes at Indian product companies

Indian firms like Meesho and Freshworks are now using AI to write a significant portion of their codebase, joining Google, Microsoft, and Meta globally. This signals that AI coding tools are moving from experiment to infrastructure at India's leading product companies. Startups building developer productivity, code review, or AI-native software tools have a narrowing window before this becomes fully commoditised.

Cybersecurity AI emerging as a critical investment theme

A WEF report highlights the shift to an 'AI vs AI' era in cybersecurity, where both attackers and defenders deploy autonomous systems. For Indian VCs, this is a strong signal to back AI-native security startups — India's large enterprise and government digital infrastructure makes it a prime market. Founders in agentic threat detection and autonomous response have a credible pitch for 2026.
