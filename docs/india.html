AI Upskilling Is a Quietly Massive Market

Coursera, Eruditus, upGrad, and Simplilearn are all reporting sharp enrollment spikes as Indian executives race to stay relevant in an AI-first economy — a signal that B2B enterprise learning is becoming a high-growth segment. VCs who missed the first edtech wave should be watching AI reskilling platforms closely, especially those targeting CXO-level and mid-management cohorts. Founders building vertical AI training products for Indian corporates are sitting on strong demand tailwinds right now.

Founder-VC Friction Goes Public — A Cautionary Signal

The OnFinance AI incident — where a Bengaluru fintech AI founder publicly called out All In Capital for passing on his startup, then apologised — is a rare but telling signal of pressure building among early-stage AI founders struggling to close rounds. As VC selectivity increases in the post-hype AI funding environment, expect more friction between founders who believe in their thesis and investors deploying capital cautiously. Founders need to separate fundraising frustration from public reputation management.

Policy Watch 4 / 6
Delhi High Court Upholds Telegram Blocking Order

The Delhi High Court ruled that the government had sufficient grounds to block Telegram, validating state authority over digital platform access in India. For AI startups relying on Telegram for community building, customer engagement, or bot-driven product delivery, this ruling underscores the regulatory risk of depending on third-party messaging platforms that can be blocked without notice.
