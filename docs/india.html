VC Trends 5 / 8
Fintech continues to vacuum up the lion's share of VC capital

With Scapia's $63M round single-handedly accounting for over 50% of this week's $122-124M total, capital concentration in fintech remains extreme. Most other rounds this week were small or mid-size, suggesting VCs are being highly selective outside of proven fintech bets. Founders in other sectors should expect longer fundraising cycles and tighter term sheets.

Smart manufacturing and industrial AI emerging as a sleeper investment theme

Rockwell's data shows Indian firms are spending 1.6x more than global peers on smart manufacturing in the high-spend bracket — a clear signal that industrial AI, automation, and IoT are accelerating faster in India than the startup ecosystem currently reflects. VCs tracking deep tech and B2B SaaS should watch this space closely for emerging founders building manufacturing intelligence tools.

Hardware-bundled AI distribution: a new go-to-market frontier

HMD's decision to bundle an Indian AI chatbot onto its smartphone is the clearest sign yet that AI distribution in India is moving beyond apps and browsers to native device integration. For early-stage Indian AI startups, OEM partnerships and device-level bundling could become a critical — and underutilized — channel to reach the next 300 million users.

Policy Watch 6 / 8
Calcutta HC Refuses to Order OpenAI to Surface IndiaMart Links on ChatGPT

The Calcutta High Court declined IndiaMART's plea demanding that ChatGPT include its links in responses, leaving unresolved the larger legal question of whether AI systems have any obligation to surface or index specific commercial platforms. This sets a cautious early precedent in India — courts appear reluctant to intervene in how AI models curate outputs, but the underlying tension between AI gatekeeping and open commerce indexing will only intensify as more Indian businesses depend on AI-driven discovery.
