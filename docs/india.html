India-focused VC funds scaling up significantly

3one4 Capital's Fund V targeting $225M signals that early-stage India-focused VCs are raising larger pools of capital, reflecting rising confidence in Indian tech and AI startups. This follows a broader trend of domestic funds growing in ambition and ticket size. Founders can expect more dry powder but also higher bars for differentiation at the early stage.

Energy and deep-tech VC institutionalizing in India

ONGC Startup Fund actively hiring senior advisors points to PSU-backed capital entering the venture ecosystem in a more structured way. Combined with Agnikul's large spacetech raise, deep-tech (space, energy, AI infrastructure) is emerging as a serious investable category beyond software. Founders building in AI-adjacent hardware or climate-tech should watch PSU-backed funds as a new source of strategic capital.

AI infrastructure as the next frontier for FDI in India

IT Minister Ashwini Vaishnaw confirming Google is 'seriously' considering manufacturing AI servers in India marks a major inflection for domestic AI infrastructure. If realized, this would reduce India's dependence on imported compute and create an anchor for a local AI hardware ecosystem. Startups building on top of affordable sovereign compute should accelerate their roadmaps.
