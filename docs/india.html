VC Trends 5 / 9
Mega rounds concentrating capital — fewer deals, bigger bets

India's tech funding hit $7.2B in H1 2026, up 12% YoY, but on fewer deals — investors are writing larger checks into proven winners rather than spreading bets thin. Sarvam's $300M round epitomizes this barbell strategy. Founders without clear differentiation or traction will find the seed-to-Series A gap brutal in this environment.

Global hyperscalers doubling down on India AI infra

Amazon's fresh $13B India commitment (on top of prior pledges) focused explicitly on AI and cloud infrastructure is a massive structural tailwind for India's AI ecosystem. This creates a rising tide for cloud-native AI startups in India — cheaper access to compute, more enterprise cloud budgets, and AWS partnership opportunities are all accelerating.

Government as a strategic LP — a new model for national AI champions

The Indian government's likely equity stake in Sarvam via the IndiaAI Mission is a landmark precedent: the state acting not just as a grant-giver or regulator but as a minority investor in private AI unicorns. This de-risks large rounds for VCs and signals which AI verticals (foundational models, sovereign AI infrastructure) have explicit government backing — a powerful signal for co-investors.

Policy Watch 6 / 9
RBI mandates AI kill switches and human oversight for banks

India's Reserve Bank of India is pushing financial institutions to implement mandatory AI risk assessments, explainability standards, emergency kill switches, and human oversight mechanisms for all deployed AI models. For fintech and AI-in-banking startups, this raises the compliance bar significantly — but also opens a market for AI governance, monitoring, and audit tooling.
