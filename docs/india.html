VC Trends 5 / 8
Deeptech gets dedicated capital allocation

Chiratae Ventures commits $10M specifically for 5 deeptech startups, betting on untapped potential in advanced technology plays. This dedicated allocation signals VCs are carving out separate buckets for high-risk, high-reward deeptech beyond vanilla SaaS, with advanced AI/ML and hardware-software convergence likely targets. Founders in robotics, semiconductor design, and AI infrastructure should note this appetite.

Fewer, bigger bets dominate Karnataka's ecosystem

Karnataka raised $868M in Q1 2026 (down 16% QoQ, up 7% YoY), with Bengaluru capturing 98% of capital. Investors are consolidating around larger check sizes rather than spraying early-stage capital. For AI founders: seed rounds are getting tougher, but growth-stage startups with traction are seeing stronger interest. Three IPOs in the quarter also suggest VCs want liquidity visibility.

AI robotics founders backing quick commerce

Lachy Groom (co-founder of AI robotics company Physical Intelligence) leading Pronto's Series B extension signals cross-pollination between AI infrastructure and hyperlocal logistics. VCs with AI/robotics expertise are betting commerce infrastructure will need autonomous systems at scale. Founders building AI-driven supply chain, warehouse automation, or delivery optimization should pitch to this cohort.
