VC Trends 7 / 11
Late-stage consolidation over early bets

VCs are writing bigger checks to fewer companies — Q1 2026 saw deal count collapse 54% even as fintech funding held at $513M. Late-stage investments are up, signaling a flight to quality and proven business models. For founders: seed capital is scarcer, but if you have traction and unit economics, growth capital is available at scale.

Infrastructure plays gaining momentum

India's data centre capacity is projected to surge nearly 6x to 10.5 GW by FY31, requiring over $60 billion in capex, with a huge chunk going to power infrastructure and renewables. VCs are betting on AI infrastructure — data centers, power solutions, and industrial automation — as the picks-and-shovels play alongside AI applications. Founders building enabling infra (not just apps) should find eager capital.

Fintech remains resilient, Mumbai leads

Despite deal count drops, fintech held funding flat at $513M in Q1, with Mumbai leading as the hub for lending and housing finance. VCs are staying in fintech but demanding scale and regulatory compliance. The message: fintech isn't dead, but only mature players with defensible moats are getting funded in this environment.

Policy Watch 8 / 11
India tests nationwide Cell Broadcast Alert System

The government tested a mass emergency alert system across India, reaching millions of phones simultaneously. For AI startups, this signals growing government focus on infrastructure resilience and public safety tech — opportunities in disaster response, predictive analytics, and citizen communication platforms.
