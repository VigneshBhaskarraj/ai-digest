VC Trends 8 / 12
India AI infrastructure bets accelerating — compute, chips, and sovereign stack

Pavestone's $5M into Tsavorite signals VCs are finally backing India's hardware/semiconductor AI layer beyond SaaS. With Pixxel launching orbital datacentres and NVIDIA pushing DGX Spark for local sovereign AI, investors see India building its own AI compute stack from edge to space. For founders: verticalized AI infrastructure (chip design, edge devices, composable architectures) is now fundable — stop pitching generic LLM wrappers.

AI-powered operational efficiency (not chatbots) is the new D2C survival playbook

FarMart hit EBITDA profitability with 50% YoY growth by deploying AI-powered workflows in agrifood supply chains. As D2C brands face manufacturing contract chaos and margin pressure, VCs are rewarding startups using AI for inventory optimization, demand forecasting, and supplier management — not consumer-facing gimmicks. The message: AI must touch your unit economics, not just your pitch deck.

Vision AI and multimodal models finding product-market fit in India's unstructured markets

Constems AI's retail execution platform highlights a pattern: computer vision solving high-friction compliance, audit, and quality control problems in fragmented supply chains (retail, manufacturing, logistics). India's unstructured markets create massive training data moats. Founders building vision AI for operations (not consumer apps) are quietly gaining traction with enterprise buyers desperate for ground-truth visibility.

Policy Watch 9 / 12
Govt eyes minimum design capability mandates for electronics manufacturing scheme participants

India is tightening Electronic Component Manufacturing Scheme rules to force companies beyond mere assembly into high-value local design work. Firms not investing in design capabilities face removal from the scheme — this directly impacts AI hardware startups and chip design ventures, signaling policy shift toward IP creation, not just production capacity.
